## Brand DNA

### Essence
Crafted luxury for the modern woman.

### Positioning Statement
Fashion meets functionality in quiet, artisanal knitwear designed for the creative professional.

### Aesthetic Codes
1. **Material Honesty**: Embrace natural textures and undyed fibers.
2. **Architectural Silhouettes**: Structured designs that mimic tailoring techniques.
3. **Raw Handwork**: Exposed seams and hand-linked details.
4. **Seasonless Elegance**: Timeless pieces with a minimalist approach.

### Tone of Voice
Refined yet relatable, thoughtful and understated. A whisper rather than a shout, always respectful of the wearer's experience and the materials used.

## Positioning & Strategy

### Target Customer
Creative professionals aged 28-40 who appreciate quality craftsmanship over flashy logos. They value authenticity and longevity in their wardrobe choices.

### Price Tier
Premium pricing, aligning with competitors like Loro Piana or Bottega Veneta, ensuring a $500-$1,000 range for key pieces such as cardigans and blazers.

### Where it Sits vs. References
Positioned between the quiet luxury of Jil Sander and The Row while distinguishing itself through structured knitwear that mimics tailoring techniques. It contrasts with athleisure trends by offering a more formal, architectural approach to knitted garments.

### Launch Channel
Start with an e-commerce platform (such as Shopify) for direct-to-consumer sales. Partner with a high-end physical boutique or two in key cities like New York and London to build credibility among early adopters.

### Seasonal Cadence
Release collections twice a year, focusing on spring/summer and fall/winter seasons, with additional drop dates for special occasions such as holidays and fashion weeks.

## Signature

### Ownable Element
Exposed hand-linked seams and undyed natural fibres. These signature details will recur in various pieces to reinforce the brand's artisanal ethos and material honesty.

## Next Steps

### 1. Develop a Brand Identity
Create a comprehensive brand identity that includes a logo, color palette (bone, oat, charcoal, faded oxblood), and typography to ensure consistency across all touchpoints.

### 2. Build an E-commerce Site
Design and launch a user-friendly e-commerce platform that showcases the collection effectively. Ensure the site is optimized for both desktop and mobile use, with clear product descriptions and high-quality images.

### 3. Establish Early Partnerships
Secure partnerships with select retail boutiques in New York and London to provide initial exposure and credibility. Leverage these partners for launch events or pop-up shops to create buzz around the brand.
